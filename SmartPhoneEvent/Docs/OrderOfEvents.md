# Order of Events

### The Setup
- Property manager scheduled vacation in The Bahamas.
- Tenant failed to pay rent.
- Property manager texted tenant; tenant claimed mother died in Chicago and promised to catch up soon.
- After vacation, property manager texted tenant again—no response.
- Property manager visited property; found a different occupant claiming to rent the unit.  Has no lease, don't know rent amount, doesn't know who he paid.
- Property manager called owner; owner instructed delivery of a 10-day notice while researching post-COVID legal options.
- New mystery person did not vacate; no signs of movement.
- Owner visited Coles County Sheriff’s Department for advice; referred to courthouse.
- Courthouse provided owner an eviction packet and hotline number.
- Owner informs property manager of current status via text before driving away.
- Owner voice initiates GPS navigation system to navigate home.

### The Event
- Owner realized he doesn't have a copy of the lease.
- Property manager texts back.  Owner reads text at stoplight that poped up over GPS.  Owner initiates a call (instead of texting) to property manager while at red light hoping to swing by and get copy of lease.
- Owner was pulled over and told vague rules about cell phone use (A constitutionally procected activity)
- Owner activates police scanner app and listens to police radio chatter.
- Officer returns and tickets owner for 625 ILCS 5/12-610.2
- Officer claims home invation is civil issue.

### Aftermath
- Owner called cops for Knock and Talk a couple times.
- Owner negotiated directly with unknown occupant; occupant eventually moved out.
- Owner reviewed statute identitied on ticket, identified exceptions.
- Owner contests ticket.
- Owner creates ai motion; failure to state a claim.
- Owner attends trial; cop doesn't show.  [Transcript](Docs/24TR732Overholt(6-18-24).transcript.pdf)
- Second court appearance: owner lost case and paid fine.  Owner told talking is as dangerous as DUI.  Judge cites "The People of the State of Illinois v. Bethany Austin".
- Owner reaches out to the ACLU, Illinois Attorney General, Chief judge Bovard for help. (maybe others, it's a blur)
- Owner reaches out to the chief judge (not Bovard) for a copy of the trial.  Denied.
